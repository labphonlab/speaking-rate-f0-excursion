## Issues requiring a decision before extraction

1. **Buckeye label normalization (parsing).** ~5.2% of `.phones` intervals carry a
   `; *`-style annotation suffix (e.g. `"ih ; *"`, `"ah; *"`) mixed in with clean
   labels (`"ih"`). Exact matching drops them. Normalizing (`split(";")[0].strip().lower()`)
   recovers ~6% more vowel tokens (11,878 → 12,616 over 12 files). **Must normalize
   labels in build_dataset**, else Buckeye N is undercounted by ~6%.

2. **Buckeye case.** Non-speech labels are UPPERCASE (`SIL`, `NOISE`, `VOCNOISE`,
   `{B_TRANS}`) but `config.pause_labels` are lowercase → **matching must be
   case-insensitive**.

3. **Buckeye nasalized vowels.** Variants like `own, ehn, ihn, aen, ahn, aan, ayn,
   iyn` (91 in the 12-file sample) are NOT in `config.vowels.en`. Decision: include
   as their base vowel, or exclude. Affects Buckeye N and comparability.

4. **CSJ Dialogue speaker attribution (measurement validity).**
   Each session has ONE TextGrid / ONE `seg` tier but TWO channel wavs (`-L`,`-R`)
   mapped to two speaker IDs. A single seg tier annotates only ONE talker. Seg
   speech-coverage is sparse (0.45–0.63 of timeline), consistent with single-talker
   turn structure. Applying those segment times to BOTH channels would measure the
   real talker on one channel and cross-channel bleed/silence on the other →
   invalid. **Needs a resolution** (identify which channel the TextGrid annotates;
   or drop the two-speaker treatment; or channel-select per segment by energy).
