# Journal of Phonetics — Guide for Authors

Saved for reference during resubmission preparation (this repository is not the
version of record). Full text of the JPhon Guide for Authors as provided.

## About the journal

### Aims and scope
The Journal of Phonetics publishes papers of an experimental or theoretical nature that deal with phonetic aspects of language and linguistic communication processes. Papers dealing with technological and/or pathological topics, or papers of an interdisciplinary nature are also suitable, provided that linguistic-phonetic principles underlie the work reported. Regular articles, registered reports, technical notes, and letters to the editor are published. Review articles may be published on an invitation basis or when arranged in advance in consultation with the Editor-in-Chief.

## Ethics and policies

### Declaration of competing interests
All authors must disclose any financial and personal relationships with other people or organizations that could inappropriately influence or bias their work. Authors with no competing interests to declare should select the option "I have nothing to declare".

### Funding sources
Authors must disclose any funding sources. If no funding has been provided:
"This research did not receive any specific grant from funding agencies in the public, commercial, or not-for-profit sectors."

### Declaration of generative AI use
Authors must declare the use of generative AI tools in the manuscript preparation process upon submission.
The use of AI tools in the manuscript preparation process must be declared by adding a statement at the end of the manuscript when the paper is first submitted. **The statement will appear in the published work and should be placed in a new section before the references list.** Example:
- Title of new section: **Declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the manuscript preparation process.**
- Statement: During the preparation of this work the author(s) used [NAME OF TOOL / SERVICE] in order to [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the author(s) reviewed and edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of the published article.
The declaration does not apply to basic tools (grammar, spelling, references). If you have nothing to disclose, you do not need to add a statement.

## Writing and formatting

### File format
Editable source files required (.doc/.docx / .tex). A PDF is not acceptable. Word files in single-column layout. Remove strikethrough/underline. Use spell/grammar check.

### Title page
Article title (concise, avoid abbreviations/formulae); author names (given + family); affiliations (full postal address + country + email); corresponding author clearly indicated; present/permanent address as footnote if moved.

### Abstract
Concise and factual, **not exceeding 250 words**. State purpose, principal results, major conclusions. Must stand alone. Avoid references (if essential, cite author + year). Avoid non-standard abbreviations (define at first mention).

### Keywords
**Provide 1 to 7 keywords** for indexing. Written in English. **Avoid keywords consisting of multiple words (using "and" or "of").** Only use abbreviations if firmly established.

### Highlights
Required at submission.
- **Submit highlights as a separate editable file with the word "highlights" included in the file name.**
- **3 to 5 bullet points, each a maximum of 85 characters, including spaces.**
Highlights should capture the novel results and any new methods.

### Tables
**Tables must be submitted as editable text, not as images.** Place next to relevant text or at end. Cite all tables in text. Number consecutively by appearance. Provide captions. Table notes below the table body. Avoid vertical rules and shading within cells. Use tables sparingly (no duplication of results described elsewhere).

### Figures, images and artwork
Supplied as separate files. Cite all images in text. Number by order of appearance. Separate file per image (Figure_1, Figure_2, ...). All artwork must have a caption (brief title not on the figure + description). Halftones: TIFF/JPG/PNG min 300 dpi (single column ≥1063 px, full page 2244 px).

### Supplementary material
Accurate and relevant. Cite all supplementary files in text. Submit at the same time. Concise descriptive caption per file. Files appear online exactly as received (not typeset).

## Article structure
- Divide into clearly defined and numbered sections. Number subsections 1.1 (then 1.1.1, 1.1.2, ...), then 1.2, etc.
- Use the numbering format when cross-referencing (do not refer to "the text").
- Subsections may have a brief heading on a separate line.
- Do not include the abstract within section numbering.

### Acknowledgements
Separate section directly before the reference list. Not on the title page or as a footnote.

### Author contributions: CRediT
Corresponding authors acknowledge co-author contributions using CRediT roles (Conceptualization, Data curation, Formal analysis, Funding acquisition, Investigation, Methodology, Project administration, Resources, Software, Supervision, Validation, Visualization, Writing – original draft, Writing – review and editing).

### Appendices
Format A, B, etc. Equations Eq. (A.1); tables/figures Table A.1, Fig. A.1.

## References

### References within text
References cited must be in the reference list and vice versa. References cited in the abstract must be given in full. Avoid unpublished results / personal communications in the reference list (may mention in text). "in press" implies accepted.

### Reference style
Citations follow **APA (Publication Manual, 7th ed., 2020)**.
The reference list should be **arranged alphabetically and then chronologically**.
**More than one reference from the same author(s) in the same year must be identified by the letters 'a', 'b', 'c', etc., placed after the year of publication.**

Examples:
- Journal: Van der Geer, J., Handgraaf T., & Lupton, R. A. (2020). The art of writing a scientific article. Journal of Scientific Communications, 163, 51–59. https://doi.org/...
- Book: Strunk, W., Jr., & White, E. B. (2000). The elements of style (4th ed.). Longman (Chapter 4).
- Chapter: Mettam, G. R., & Adams, L. B. (2020). How to prepare an electronic version of your article. In B. S. Jones, & R. Z. Smith (Eds.), Introduction to the electronic age (pp. 281–304). E-Publishing Inc.
- Dataset: add [dataset] immediately before the reference.
- Preprint: include "preprint" or the preprint server name + DOI.

## Submitting your manuscript

### Submission checklist
- One corresponding author with full contact details.
- All files uploaded (artwork, videos, supplementary), including tables, footnotes, captions.
- Spelling/grammar checks done.
- All in-text references cited in the reference list and vice versa.
- Permissions obtained for copyrighted material.

### Language
Write in American or British English but not a combination of both.
