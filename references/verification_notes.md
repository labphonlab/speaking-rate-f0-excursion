# Reference verification notes

Provenance and verification status for the 24 works cited in
"Speaking rate compresses F0 excursion, not F0 maximum, in spontaneous speech."
Keys refer to `references.bib`.

## 1. Citations verified in detail this project

| Key | Verification |
|---|---|
| `ladd1999` | **Primary source obtained and read in full** (JASA 106(3), 1543–1554). A prior draft had cited this paper inconsistently (once as undershoot support, once as evidence against alignment effects); direct reading confirmed its actual finding — rate strongly affects the *duration* of accentual F0 rises but has only small, inconsistent effects on *alignment* and *excursion size* — and the manuscript was corrected to use it accurately (segmental-anchoring / target-invariance evidence). |
| `gay1981` | **Content re-verified this session** (Phonetica 38(1–3), 148–158). The abstract/summary confirm Gay's argument that speech-rate changes reflect a *reorganization of motor strategies* (altering articulatory displacement, velocity, and coarticulation together), i.e. not a passive, time-proportional undershoot. The Introduction sentence citing Gay was refined accordingly. |
| `whalen1995` | **Primary source confirmed** (J. Phonetics 23, 349–366). Cited as the basis for treating the vowel random effect as a control for intrinsic vowel F0; wording refined to "intrinsic pitch differences between vowel categories." |
| `thart1981` | Confirmed as the classic source for pitch-distance JND (~0.5–1.0 st); used as the static perceptual benchmark in Methods §2.6.2. |
| `tanner2017` | Confirmed to use a mean-deviation (within/between) decomposition of speech rate for coronal stop deletion — the conceptual precedent for the Mundlak decomposition here. In-text citations unified to "Tanner et al. (2017)." |
| `sugito1982` | Bibliography cross-checked against an independent source (the *ososagari* / peak-delay literature), which corroborates the same lineage (Neustupný 1966 first observed peak delay; Sugito 1982 detailed it). |
| `arvaniti2026` | Confirmed: *Journal of Phonetics* 114, 101459 (2026 intonation review). |
| `maekawa2003`, `pitt2005`, `maekawa2002` | Corpus / annotation references added and verified against the corpora's own documentation (CSJ design; Buckeye labeling; X-JToBI). |
| `ishihara2003`, `ishihara2006` | Author's own works (ICPhS 2003; University of Edinburgh doctoral dissertation); self-citations for the *ososagari* structural fact and the elicited-speech alignment result. |
| `rietveld1985` | Confirmed as the source for the ~1.5 st prominence threshold used as the stricter movement-specific benchmark. |

## 3. Works considered but NOT cited (and why)

- **Koreman, J. (2006).** Perceived speech rate (JASA 119(1), 582–596). Not used:
  it had become a source of second-hand ("孫引き") attributions in earlier drafts;
  direct claims are instead grounded in primary sources.
- **Rietveld, A. C. M., & Gussenhoven, C. (1987).** Perceived speech rate and
  intonation (J. Phonetics 15, 273–285). Not used: it is a **perception** study
  (intonational complexity → perceived rate) and does not support a **production**
  claim about rate simplifying pitch accents; retained only to flag the risk of
  mis-attribution. (Note: the 1985 paper `rietveld1985` IS used and is distinct.)
- **Silverman, K., & Pierrehumbert, J. (1990).** Cited only second-hand in a prior
  source for a "small but significant" F0-peak shift under rate; **primary source
  not confirmed**, so not cited.
- **Xu, Y. (1998).** Cited second-hand for rate-invariant F0-peak alignment;
  **primary source not confirmed**, so not cited.
- **Snijders, T. A. B., & Bosker, R. J. (2011).** *Multilevel analysis* (2nd ed.).
  Listed by Tanner et al. (2017) as the textbook source of the mean-deviation
  method; edition/volume details unverified and the method is cited via Tanner et
  al. rather than the textbook, so not used.

## 4. External-review flags checked and judged NOT to require change

- **`strik1995` (Strik & Boves, 1995).** An external review suggested this citation
  was misused. On checking, the paper is a study of **subglottal pressure (Psb) and
  F0 downtrend** — it concerns the physiological relationship between subglottal
  pressure, laryngeal control, and F0, not declination alone. It is cited here
  (with `titze1989`) precisely for that subglottal-pressure / laryngeal-tension
  relationship underlying the floor-raising mechanism (§4.3), which is an accurate
  use. **No change made.**
- **`ishihara2006` (Ishihara, 2006).** Checked for correct characterization
  (controlled fast-vs-normal Japanese speech; rate shifted the F0 peak later but
  the shift did not reach significance). The manuscript's use in §4.6 matches the
  dissertation's Experiment 2 finding. **No change made.**
